Ingredients:

Miso paste:
1 Small yellow onion
6 cloves of garlic
250ml. (1 cup) of white miso
3 tablespoons of chili paste ( I use Sambal Oelek)
5cm (2 in) of fresh ginger
3 tablespoons Mirin sweetened sake
2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
1 tablespoon of sesame oil

Soup:
400gr (14 oz.) of extra firm tofu
Vegetable oil
150gr (5 oz.) Shiitake mushrooms
700ml. (3 cups) of vegetable broth
250ml. (1 cup) of mushroom broth
500ml. (2 cups) of unsweetened soy milk
Ramen Noodles

Garnish:
1 egg per bowl (or no egg to make it vegan)
Seaweed
Cilantro
Scallions

Load in all the ingredients for miso paste into a food-possessor: 1 small onion (quartered), 6 cloves of garlic (peeled), 250ml. (1 cup) of white miso, 3 tablespoons of chili paste, 5cm (2 in) of ginger (peeled and cut), 3 tablespoons of Mirin sweetened sake, 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil, 1 tablespoon of sesame oil.
Then, pulse all the ingredients in a food-possessor until they are smoothly blended.

Dice tofu. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in your soup pot and fry the tofu until browned. Then, add 60ml. (1/4 cup) of the spicy miso paste and fry together for 2-5 minutes. Remove your tofu and set it aside.

Dice mushrooms into small pieces. After youâ€™ve removed your tofu, use the same pot and heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil, then fry your mushrooms. After the mushrooms are sufficiently browned, add 60ml. (1/4 cup) of the spicy miso paste and fry it together for 2-5 minutes.

Now add 700ml. (3 cups) of vegetable broth, 250ml. (1 cup) of mushroom broth, 500ml. (2 cups) of unsweetened soy milk to your pot with your mushrooms. Bring broth to a simmer. Put 170g (1/2 cup) of miso paste into a sieve and dissolve into the broth stirring with a spoon. Discard what does not make it through the sieve.

You can make your eggs as soft-boiled as you like. I boiled mine for 7 minutes. Boil eggs for 5 minutes to get perfect soft-boiled eggs.

Cook your ramen noodles according to the instructions on the box in a separate pot. Put the cooked noodles into bowls.

On top of the ramen noodles, pour your spicy broth, add as much tofu as you like on top. Finally, garnish with strips of seaweed, chopped cilantro, scallions, and a soft boiled egg.
