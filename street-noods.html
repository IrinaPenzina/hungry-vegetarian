Shanghai Street Noods

Shanghai street noodles have a very special place in my heart. My love for it is unconditional. There's nothing that can beat the best food in the world. This is our humble attempt to bring Shanghai Street Noodles to life on the opposite side of the world.

Share recipe

Ingredients for one bowl:

1/4 of Onion
1 egg
1 Handfull of Bok Choy leaves
1 Handfull of Mung Bean Sprouts
A pinch (or 2) of Pickled Mustard Greens
A pinch (or 2) of Spicy Pickled Bamboo Shoots Slices
A bowl of cooked Egg Noodles
1/4 ladle of Soy Sauce
1/4 a teaspoon Sambal Oelek sauce (1/2 tsp if you like it extra spicy)
(Substitute: if you don’t have Sambal Oelek sauce
feel free to use chili powder or crushed red pepper)
Garlic Powder
Vegetable oil
Optional: A few Vegan Prawns

Add to your bowl: Green onions
(NOTE: Don’t feel handcuffed to your measurements.
Throwing this together should feel wild and improvised.)

You want to cut the Bok Choy's bottom to have separate the leaves. Dice your onion. If you scroll through the pictures, you can see how the Pickled Mustard Greens, Pickled Bamboo Shoots, Mung Bean Sprouts, and Vegan Prawns look. As a side note, I regret adding those Vegan Prawns. They didn't taste good. However, you might find good ones.

You want to cook your noodles before you start frying the ingredients. Follow the instructions on the box. It's better to use egg noodles, but you can use any other noodles too. Don't salt it.

The best pan to cook in is the wok. Add vegetable oil and heat the wok on high heat. Your oil and wok should be very hot. Break an egg and beat it. Add some onion. Mix and toss it together rapidly. Try to constantly be stirring and tossing. If you want to add some plant-based meat or plant-based prawns now is the best time. All ingredients should turn brownish before you add other ingredients.

Throw the pickles and the rest of the vegetables into the wok. Rapidly stir and toss until they look soft. Throw in your noodles. into the vegetables. Continue stirring and tossing. Add soy sauce, sprinkle in some garlic powder, and stir in the Sambal Oelek sauce.

Add some chopped green onions on top of your noodles. It turned out really good.
