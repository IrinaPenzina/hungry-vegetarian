What-the-Heck’s in My Pantry Vegetarian Chili

An extremely delicious Chili! To cook this meal, we used almost all the random cans from the pantry that had been sitting there for years.

Share recipe

Ingredients:

1 Onion
1 Head of Garlic
1 Poblano Pepper
1 can of Pinto Beans - Frijotes Pintos
1 can of Kidney Beans
1 can of Sweet Corn - Cream Style (non-cream style is probably better, but creamed corn was what was in the pantry.)
1 can of Chili Mild Beans
1 can of Chiles Chipotles (We only used 2 chipotle peppers. Feel free to add more if you want more spice)
2 cans of Black Beans- rinsed
1/3 of a can Jalapeno Peppers
1 can of Diced Tomatoes
1 can of Pieces & Stems Mushrooms
1 can of Tomato Sauce
A1 steak sauce (splash in as much as you like)
2 cups of Vegetable Broth
2 table spoons -ish of Worcestershire sauce
2 tablespoons lime juice/ 1 lime juiced
Vegetable oil

Spices: 1 Tablespoon Cumin, 1 Tablespoon Chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon of Sugar, (the rest of these were used pretty liberally) Smoked Paprika, Cayenne Pepper, Salt, Barbecue Seasoning, Tajin
Add to your bowl: Parmesan Cheese, Sour Cream.

We cleared the entire pantry and used all the canned beans we could find. You don't have to use exactly the same cans of beans we did.

Chop up onions and fry them in vegetable oil. Chop up the garlic. When the onions are translucent and browned, add in the garlic with the cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and sugar. Fry for 2 more minutes.

Open up all the cans, first rinse your beans and dump them into the pot. Dice 2 adobe chipotle canned peppers. Add them into the pot. Mix it and let it cook for a minute. Add the rest of your cans.

Next, add the can of tomato sauce, 2 cups of vegetable broth, two table spoons-ish of Worcestershire sauce, liberally add barbecue seasoning, tajin, A1 steak sauce, 2 tablespoons lime juice (Or squeeze one lime. Watch your seeds.). Dice the poblano pepper and add it into the pot. Let it simmer stirring occasionally.

(Optional): We baked potatoes to top with our chili. However, the chili stands on its own. Poke the potatoes all around with a fork. Rub them in olive oil and salt. Preheat your oven to 450 degrees F. Bake potatoes for 45-60 minutes. The skin is going to be crispy.

Cut open potatoes and pour your chili on top of it. Top with some parmesan cheese and sour cream. It turns out just a little bit spicy. If you want it to be spicier add more jalapeno peppers or chipotles.
Remember, you don’t need to make this with the same cans that we did. Feel free the empty out your pantry and experiment! This recipe may be different every time you make it.
