Vegetarian Dumplings

Vegetarian Dumplings that I love to make with my husband. It’s not just a delicious meal, but also a time wonderfully spent together. It is a great family tradition for a holiday to pull everyone together.

Share recipe

Dumpling dough recipe:

1 kilo (35 oz) - Whole wheat flour;
2 - Chicken eggs;
400 ml (1.7 Cups) - Hot water;
Salt

I like to mix my dough in a bowl. First, add some flour, eggs and mix them. Next, gradually add salted hot water and keep stirring. The dough is supposed to look smooth and be quite elastic.
Cover it with a towel and let it rest for 30 min.

Now is the best time to work on your fillings. I love to make fried mushrooms with onions, mashed potatoes, cheese and use plant-based meat. However, you can fill your dumpling with anything you'd like.

Take a chunk of the dough and roll it out. Don't roll it too thin. Cut dough into even circles. Mix the leftovers back into the dough. Repeat.
I usually use a cookie cutter to make circles.

On top of the dough circle add some filling. The most important thing is to make sure you stick the edges together very securely. Otherwise, your dumplings might open while boiling, and the filling will come out.

I love my dumplings with soup. Fry some onions and garlic in the vegetable oil until they turn gold. Add the onions and garlic into a pot of boiling water with the raw dumplings. Now, add your favorite spices. I usually add some fresh dill, parsley, some dried bay leaves, peppercorns, etc.
Boil for 10 minutes.
Serve in a large bowl! As for the sauce, I usually use sour cream.
