Russian Potato Salad - Olivier - Vegetarian

It is my most favorite dish! Not only because it tastes so delicious, but also because it's a traditional dish that we eat when we celebrate New Year's in Russia. It reminds me of home and family.

Share recipe

Ingredients:

10 Eggs
10 medium-sized Potatoes
4 medium-sized Carrots
1 medium-sized Onion
Around 10 Gherkins (*pickles)
1 can of Peas
Salt
Mayonnaise

First, cook the eggs, potatoes, and carrots.
Put the eggs into cold water and wait until it boils. Set a timer for 10 minutes.
Don’t peel your potatoes and carrots. Keep the skin on them. Put whole potatoes and carrots into the same pot of cold water then boil them until they are soft.
While they are cooking, start dicing your onions.

Upon moving to the U.S., I’ve found that it's not that easy to find a can of NOT sweet peas. Don’t give up! Keep looking! If you absolutely can’t find anything but sweet peas, here is what you can do. Drain the water from the can and fill the can with pickle juice from your Gherkins Jar. Soak the sweet peas in the pickle juice for about 10 - 15 minutes. It's going to make them saltier.

Dice the pickles into small cubes. Usually, I cut around ten gherkins at the beginning and a couple more as I finish the salad. It just always seems like there aren’t enough pickles for me. I love pickles!

After 10 minutes, when the timer for your eggs goes off, drain the hot water from the pot. Refill the pot with cold water and soak the eggs until they are cool.
As the potatoes and carrots finish cooking, check to see if they are soft with a fork. If easily stabbed, then turn off the heat and drain the water from the pot. Set them aside and let them cool down a bit. It's going to be easier to peel them if they are not super-hot.

Peel the potatoes and carrots. Shell your eggs. Dice the potatoes, carrots and eggs into small pieces like you did for all the ingredients before.

Mix all ingredients except for the mayonnaise. Add some salt. I usually only mix in the mayonnaise right before serving. Mix in as much mayonnaise as you like, so it looks creamy.
The salad will last and taste better longer if you store it without the mayonnaise mixed in. However, we’ve never really had time to check my theory. We always finish it too quickly.
