Vegetarian Stuffed Peppers

Vegetarian Stuffed Peppers are just like regular stuffed peppers but better. I used plant-based meat instead of animal one and it turned out absolutely delicious.

Share recipe

Ingredients:

6 Peppers
1 Onion
5 Garlic Cloves
16 oz (453 g) of Plant-Based Ground Meat (I used Beyond Beef)
16 oz (453 g) of Rice
1/2 a teaspoon Sambal Oelek sauce (1 tsp if you like it extra spicy)
1/2 or 1 table spoon of tomato paste
Vegetable Oil
24 oz (680 g) of Pasta Sauce

Add to your plate: Parsley, Cilantro, Dill, Sour Cream.

The first thing that I do is remove the guts from my peppers. Of course, it's not a must, but it's something that puts me in the mood for cooking stuffed peppers.

Heat up vegetable oil in the pan. Dice your onion into small pieces and fry them until they turn gold. Add plant-based meat and fry until it falls apart.

It doesn't really matter how you cook your rice. I usually boil it in the pot on the stove.

Once the rice is ready, add it to the meat, mix it well, and then fry it together. Salt the mix, add tomato paste and Sambal Oelek sauce. Mix everithing well and fry for another 5 minutes.

Stuff peppers with the mix from the pan. Oil the baking sheet and lay out your peppers. Pour pasta sauce into your sheet.

Pre heat your oven to 350°F. Cover your peppers with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 20 - 30 minutes until peppers are soft.

I serve a stuffed pepper with a scoop of sour cream and a sprinkling of parsley or/and cilantro or/and dill. It is one of the meals I make that my husband compliments even after eating it again the next day.
