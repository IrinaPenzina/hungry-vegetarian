Meatless Borsch

Cheap, delicious, healthy! All in one - meet Borsch! This is one of my favorite soups in the world. No one has ever said this soup is just “okay” after trying it!

Share recipe

Ingredients:

2 Beets
2 Carrots
1 medium-size white cabbage
7 Potatoes
1 Onion
1 Head of Garlic
Vegetable oil
Salt
Spices: Bay leaves, Whole Black Peppercorns
Add to your bowl: Parsley, Cilantro, Dill, Sour Cream.

I will leave this picture here to give you an idea of how big or small the vegetables I use in my soup are. Usually, with these ingredients, you will get a BIG pot of soup. If you want a smaller pot of soup, cut everything in half.

First, put a pot of water on the stove. Start by slicing the cabbage. Try to cut it thin. As the water begins to boil, put in the cabbage and bay leaves. Bring back to a boil.

Peel and dice the potatoes. As the water with the cabbage begins to boil again, add the potatoes and peppercorns. Boil for 15 minutes.

Dice the onion into small pieces. Add the onion to a frying pan and fry them in the vegetable oil. Shred the carrots and toss them in with the onions. Next, chop the beets into small cubes and add them to the frying pan as well. Dice the garlic into small pieces and add them to the frying pan last.

Add a bit of the soup from the pot to your vegetables frying in the frying pan. Salt it and add any other spices you like. Cover and stew for 15 minutes.

Throw the stew into the pot with the cabbage and potatoes and bring back to a boil. As you wait, chop your fresh dill, parsley, and cilantro. Turn off the heat and serve the soup in a bowl with a handful of the mix of fresh herbs. Add a dollop of sour cream if you like!
