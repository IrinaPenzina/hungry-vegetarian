Cabbage Stew

No person hasn't tried Cabbage Stew. In my opinion, there are four main ingredients: cabbage, potatoes, carrots, and onions. As for the rest, use whatever vegetables you like. Here's my version of the well-known cabbage stew.

Share recipe

Ingredients:
1/2 medium cabbage
1 medium onion
3 medium potatoes
2 medium carrots
5 garlic cloves
6 oz of white mushrooms
1 tomato
2 broccoli
2 cups of water
3 oz of tomato paste
3 bay leaves
Vegetable oil
Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
Parsley

I like to cut all the vegetables before I put the pan on the stove. But it's a question of your preference.
Start by warming up the oil in the pan. Throw diced onion in and fry it until it gets a little golden. Next, sliced mushrooms go in and make sure you fry them until their edges turn gold.

Now, as you see the golden edges of your mushrooms, it's time to add more vegetables.
Add shredded carrots, diced potatoes, sliced tomatoes, shredded garlic, little broccoli trees separated from the stem, salt and pepper.
Stir and let sit 5-7 minutes.

Well, after 5-7 minutes add a cup of water with tomato pasted mixed in it. Add bay leaves. As it boils add sliced cabbage and close the lid.
In 10-15 minutes add another cup of water with tomato pasted in it. Close the lid again.

Let it sit for another 10-15 minutes. Check and see to make sure that all the vegetables are soft. As the stew is ready add some parsley on top for the color.

Here it is cabbage stew on the plate. Cheap and easy.
